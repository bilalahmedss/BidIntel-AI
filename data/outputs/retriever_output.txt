CHUNK 1: Nexus Advisory - Selected Completed Projects
Sector Focus: Digital Financial Services, Payment Systems, Financial Inclusion

Project 1: Merchant Discount Rate (MDR) Study and Policy Reform Options
Client: Central Bank of Lumina (Southeast Asia) [fictionalized]
Duration: 7 months (Completed)
Contract Type: Technical Assistance - Strategy and Policy

Background
The central bank sought an evidence-based review of MDR structures to increase digital merchant acceptance without undermining provider sustainability. Merchant adoption had stagnated in non-metro areas, and market participants reported inconsistent pricing, low transparency, and weak incentives for small merchants.

Objectives
- Assess current MDR structures by channel (card-present, QR, account-to-account)
- Quantify merchant economics by segment (micro, small, medium, large)
- Evaluate consumer usage patterns and transaction-cost sensitivity
- Recommend phased MDR reforms with risk mitigation and implementation sequencing

Methodology
- Review of central bank circulars, scheme rules, and provider pricing schedules
- Analysis of transaction data across issuing/acquiring institutions
- Structured interviews with banks, payment service providers, fintechs, and merchant associations
- International benchmark review (peer ASEAN and emerging markets)
- Scenario modeling for merchant growth and provider margin implications

Key Deliverables
- MDR baseline diagnostics and market segmentation report
- Reform options paper with 3 phased policy pathways
- Implementation roadmap with governance roles, timelines, and KPIs
- Monitoring dashboard specification for post-reform tracking

Outcomes
- Central bank adopted a phased MDR approach for priority merchant categories
- Policy package included transparency requirements and small-merchant onboarding incentives
- Program implementation moved into regulatory consultation phase with broad stakeholder buy-in

Relevance to RFP Signals
- Demonstrated central bank project experience
- Strong quantitative and qualitative research capability
- Proven stakeholder engagement and policy drafting support
- Clear implementation planning and results framework design

Project 2: Digital Payments Ecosystem Assessment
Client: East Africa Inclusive Finance Initiative (Gates Foundation-funded) [fictionalized program title]
Duration: 9 months (Completed)
Contract Type: Advisory - Ecosystem and Program Design

Background
The program aimed to accelerate inclusive digital payments in underserved regions by identifying structural barriers across policy, infrastructure, business models, and user trust. The funder required actionable recommendations linked to implementation feasibility and measurable outcomes.

Objectives
- Map the end-to-end payments ecosystem across regulators, providers, agents, merchants, and end users
- Diagnose constraints affecting rural and women-led microenterprise adoption
- Assess interoperability readiness and acceptance infrastructure gaps
- Define a practical intervention portfolio with budget envelopes and sequencing

Methodology
- Multi-country desk review and in-country consultation workshops
- Stakeholder mapping and influence-interest analysis
- Supply-side and demand-side data synthesis
- Use-case walkthroughs (G2P, merchant payments, remittances, value chain payments)
- Design of program M&E framework with baseline and target indicators

Key Deliverables
- Comprehensive ecosystem assessment report
- Prioritized intervention portfolio and implementation sequencing plan
- Risk register including regulatory, operational, and market risks
- Program governance and accountability framework

Outcomes
- Program steering committee approved a two-year implementation package
- Recommended interventions were integrated into annual work planning
- M&E dashboard and reporting cadence institutionalized for donor and partner oversight

Relevance to RFP Signals
- Experience in donor-funded payment ecosystem diagnostics
- Ability to work across public and private stakeholders
- Strong inclusion lens and evidence-based policy recommendations
- Delivery of actionable outputs: roadmaps, KPIs, and governance structures
